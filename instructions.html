Tier 1 — Build These First (Week 1–2)
Core Injured Worker Calculators
Highest search volume · Acute user need · Biggest traffic driver
#01 Build First
Weekly Benefit Calculator
The #1 searched workers' comp tool. Injured workers need to know "how much will I get per week?" The answer is state-specific (usually 66.67% of AWW, capped by state max). This is your homepage hero tool.
RPM: $40–60 Users: Injured Workers Difficulty: Low
Inputs needed
  • State (dropdown)
  • Gross weekly wage (pre-injury)
  • Injury date (determines which year's caps apply)
  • Disability type: Total / Partial
#02 Build First
Settlement Value Estimator
The second most-searched tool. "What is my workers' comp case worth?" Calculates estimated settlement range based on injury severity, wages, medical costs, and state rules. Generates intense ad demand — settlement attorneys pay $20–60 CPC.
RPM: $45–65 Users: Injured Workers Difficulty: Medium
Inputs needed
  • State
  • Average weekly wage
  • Injury type (select from list)
  • Weeks out of work
  • Total medical bills to date
  • Impairment rating % (if known)
  • Permanent disability: Yes / No
#03 Build First
Average Weekly Wage (AWW) Calculator
Before workers can calculate their benefit, they need their AWW. This sounds simple but is surprisingly confusing — do overtime count? Multiple jobs? Bonuses? This calculator walks them through it step by step.
RPM: $35–50 Users: Injured Workers Difficulty: Low
Inputs needed
  • Pay period type: Weekly / Bi-weekly / Semi-monthly / Monthly
  • Gross wages for last 52 weeks (or last 13 pay stubs)
  • Overtime hours (include or exclude toggle)
  • Multiple jobs? Yes/No + second job wages
  • Bonuses / commissions in past year
#04 Build First
Permanent Partial Disability (PPD) Calculator
Once a worker reaches MMI and gets an impairment rating, they need to know what their PPD benefits are worth. State formulas vary wildly — some use body part schedules, some use impairment %, some use age + wage. Deeply needed, poorly served online.
RPM: $40–60 Users: Injured Workers Difficulty: Medium
Inputs needed
  • State
  • Impairment rating % (from doctor)
  • Body part injured (select)
  • Average weekly wage
  • Age at time of injury
  • Able to return to same job? Yes/No
#05 Build First
Filing Deadline Checker
Statute of limitations varies by state (1–7 years). Workers are terrified of missing the deadline. This is a simple but extremely high-anxiety tool: enter state + injury date → shows exactly how many days they have left to file, with a countdown urgency element.
RPM: $40–55 Users: Injured Workers Difficulty: Low
Inputs needed
  • State
  • Date of injury (date picker)
  • Claim type: Injury / Occupational disease
  • Did you notify employer? Yes/No + date

Tier 2 — Build Next (Week 3–5)
Deeper Claim & Benefit Calculators
Strong search intent · Medium complexity · Reinforces topical authority
#06 High Priority
Impairment Rating Payout Calculator
Takes a doctor-assigned impairment rating % and translates it into dollars. Many states pay a set number of "benefit weeks" per % point. Users with a freshly assigned rating frantically search for what it means in money.
RPM: $40–58 Users: Injured Workers Difficulty: Medium
Inputs needed
  • State
  • Impairment rating % (0–100)
  • Average weekly wage
  • Body part (for scheduled award states)
  • Date of injury (some states use different multipliers by year)
#07 High Priority
Lost Wages Calculator
Calculates total lost wages over the entire claim period — not just weekly rate, but cumulative loss. Useful for workers understanding total claim value and for those returning to light duty with reduced pay (partial disability scenario).
RPM: $35–50 Users: Injured Workers Difficulty: Low
Inputs needed
  • Pre-injury weekly wage
  • Current weekly wage (if on light duty)
  • State
  • Start date of disability
  • End date (or "ongoing" toggle)
  • Benefit rate received
#08 High Priority
State Weekly Benefit Cap Lookup
Every state sets a maximum weekly workers' comp benefit (e.g., California's is $1,620/week in 2024). Workers with higher wages need to know if they're hitting the cap. Simple lookup but extremely frequently searched — works as a reference table + calculator hybrid.
RPM: $30–45 Users: Injured Workers Difficulty: Low
Inputs needed
  • State
  • Year of injury (caps update annually)
  • Your AWW (to show if you're capped)
#09 High Priority
Waiting Period Calculator
Most states have a 3–7 day waiting period before benefits start. Some retroactively pay that period if disability lasts beyond a threshold. Injured workers constantly ask "when do my checks start?" — this answers it precisely.
RPM: $32–48 Users: Injured Workers Difficulty: Low
Inputs needed
  • State
  • First day unable to work
  • Expected days out (estimate)
#10 High Priority
Vocational Rehabilitation Benefit Estimator
Workers who can't return to their old job may be entitled to vocational rehab benefits — covering retraining, education, and during-training wage support. Few resources explain what this is worth. Unique niche within the niche.
RPM: $35–50 Users: Injured Workers Difficulty: Medium
Inputs needed
  • State
  • Current AWW
  • Can return to same job? Yes/No
  • Retraining program duration (months)
  • Estimated retraining costs
#11 High Priority
Death Benefits Calculator
For fatal workplace injuries. Families need to understand burial benefits, ongoing weekly benefits for dependents, and duration. Extremely high-stakes search intent. Funeral homes and wrongful death attorneys advertise here — very high CPC.
RPM: $45–65 Users: Surviving Family Difficulty: Medium
Inputs needed
  • State
  • Deceased worker's AWW
  • Number of dependents
  • Ages of dependent children
  • Surviving spouse? Yes/No

Tier 3 — Employer-Facing Tools (Month 2)
Insurance & Premium Calculators
Second audience = double traffic · Insurance advertisers pay premium CPC
#12 Employer Tool
Workers' Comp Premium Estimator
Small business owners need to estimate their annual workers' comp insurance cost. Formula: (Payroll ÷ 100) × Class Rate × Experience Modifier. Insurance broker ads on this page pay $8–25 CPC. This is the top employer-side calculator.
RPM: $35–55 Users: Employers Difficulty: Medium
Inputs needed
  • State
  • Industry / job type (select NCCI class code category)
  • Annual payroll ($)
  • Number of employees
  • Experience modifier (optional — explain what it is)
#13 Employer Tool
Experience Modification (E-Mod) Calculator
The experience modifier (e-mod) directly multiplies the employer's premium — a 1.2 mod means 20% more expensive insurance. Employers with poor safety records search desperately to understand their mod and how to reduce it. Unique, high-value tool.
RPM: $30–50 Users: Employers / HR Difficulty: High
Inputs needed
  • State
  • 3 years of payroll data
  • 3 years of claims data (losses)
  • NCCI class code
  • Current e-mod (if known)
#14 Employer Tool
Workers' Comp Coverage Requirement Checker
Many small business owners don't know if they're required to carry workers' comp. Rules vary by state — some require it with 1 employee, others at 3+ employees, some exempt certain industries. This is a simple but extremely searched compliance tool.
RPM: $28–45 Users: Employers Difficulty: Low
Inputs needed
  • State
  • Number of employees (FT + PT)
  • Business type / industry
  • Are any workers contractors? Yes/No
  • Any workers seasonal? Yes/No
#15 Employer Tool
Payroll Audit Estimator
Workers' comp premiums are based on estimated payroll, then audited at year end. Employers who underestimate owe a big check. This calculator helps employers project their audit result and avoid surprises — a very specific pain point with strong search intent.
RPM: $28–42 Users: Employers / Finance Difficulty: Low
Inputs needed
  • Estimated payroll at policy start
  • Actual payroll at year end
  • Class code rate
  • Experience modifier

Tier 4 — Specialized Tools (Month 2–3)
Niche Calculators with Long-Tail SEO
Lower volume but very high intent · Less competition · Strong internal linking value
#16 Specialized
Body Part Injury Value Calculator
Most states have a "schedule of injuries" — a table assigning a fixed number of benefit weeks to each body part (e.g., loss of thumb = 60 weeks). Users who know their body part and wage can calculate their scheduled award instantly. Very long-tail, very intent-driven.
RPM: $38–55 Users: Injured Workers Difficulty: Medium
Inputs needed
  • State
  • Body part injured (detailed list: finger, toe, thumb, arm, leg, eye, hearing, etc.)
  • Amputation vs. partial loss
  • Loss percentage (if partial)
  • Average weekly wage
#17 Specialized
Social Security Offset Calculator
If a worker receives both workers' comp AND Social Security Disability (SSDI), there's an "offset" — SSA may reduce SSDI payments. This is a complex calculation that almost nobody explains clearly online. Very low competition, very high intent.
RPM: $35–52 Users: Injured Workers on SSDI Difficulty: High
Inputs needed
  • Monthly workers' comp benefit
  • Monthly SSDI benefit (before offset)
  • Average current monthly earnings (if working)
  • Number of dependents receiving SSDI
#18 Specialized
Employer Penalty & Fine Estimator
Employers operating without required workers' comp face massive fines — sometimes $10K+ per employee per day uninsured. An employer who just got a compliance notice searches frantically for "how much will I be fined." Scary intent = high engagement = great RPM.
RPM: $30–48 Users: Employers (scared) Difficulty: Low
Inputs needed
  • State
  • Number of uninsured employees
  • Number of days uninsured (approx.)
  • Any workplace injuries during uninsured period? Yes/No
#19 Specialized
Nurse Case Manager Cost Estimator
Insurance companies often assign nurse case managers to complex claims, and their costs can be challenged by workers. Helps workers and employers understand what these services cost, whether they're appropriate, and how they affect the claim timeline.
RPM: $28–42 Users: Workers / Employers Difficulty: Low
Inputs needed
  • State
  • Claim type / injury severity
  • Estimated claim duration (weeks)
#20 Specialized
Attorney Fee Calculator
Workers' comp attorney fees are regulated by state — usually 10–20% of the settlement, capped at a state maximum. Workers want to know what their attorney will take before signing. Ironically, this page will attract attorney ads at $15–40 CPC. Perfect alignment.
RPM: $42–65 Users: Injured Workers Difficulty: Low
Inputs needed
  • State
  • Estimated settlement value ($)
  • Fee structure: % of settlement vs. hourly
  • Has case gone to hearing? Yes/No (affects max fee in some states)

The Strategy in One Sentence

Build tools for the two people who desperately need answers: the injured worker who just got hurt and is terrified, and the small business owner who doesn't know if they're compliant. Both audiences have legal advertisers paying $10–60 CPC targeting them.

None of these calculations are simple enough for an AI search answer. They all require state-specific inputs, real-time data, and interactivity. That's your moat.
